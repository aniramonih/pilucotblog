Career

Abigail Kubeka has been featured in numerous musicals, such as Ma’s Got the Blues, The Best Little Whorehouse in Texas, Ain’t Misbehaving, Six of the Best, Two Women of Africa, and King Afrika, the list goes on.

She has made a debut in several television shows, such as Sewende Laan, Soul City, Generations, BBC’s Holby City, and, most recently, BBC’s Wild at Home. She also starred in Cry, the Beloved Country.

Her voice has taken her all around Africa, and she has performed at countless prestigious cabarets. In 1981, Abigail Kubeka‘s debut album was released in Nigeria and Europe.

During the queen of England’s visit to South Africa in 1995, she got the opportunity to perform some of her songs for her. She also performed at the All Africa Games opening ceremony in Johannesburg in 1999 and at the South African Embassy in London during the country’s 10th-anniversary celebrations in 2004.

Abigail Kubeka has won several awards and has been named Soweto Top 100 influential People, and she also made the City Press Top 10 Women Achievers lists. 

She was one of the 84 African women honored with the Women of Our Time award in 1986.
