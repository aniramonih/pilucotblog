Early Life & Biography

Till was born in Leipzig. His father, Werner Lindemann, has been a poet. His mother Brigitte Hildegard “Gitta” Lindemann had been a journalist who has served for Norddeutscher Rundfunk from 1992 to 2002. Till was raised in Wendisch-Rambow. He has a younger sister named Saskia. When he was 11, he went to Empor Rostock Sports Club that was ranked among the good sports schools of that time. Later, from 1970 to 1980, he also attended a boarding school.

After 1975, his parents lived in different relations due to their work commitments. Their relationship eventually led to divorce at the time when Lindemann was young. Though he was with his father for only a few years, whatever time he has spent with him was not pleasant. It was an unhealthy relationship. Till’s father has talked about his personal life in the book “Mike Oldfield,” where he wrote that he had struggled due to alcoholism and had faced difficulties being a father of his teenage son, Till Lindemann.

In 1978, Till stood at the eleventh position in the European Junior Swimming Championships that was held in Florence. He also got shortlisted for the 1980 Olympics that took place in Moscow. Due to getting injured, he had to leave the sport till has worked as a trainee carpenter, a meat cutter, a basket weaver, and also a gallery technician. Till later made a transition to the music industry. His first album was released as Stormbringer.

Career

In 1986, Till began his career in music by playing drums for a rock band named “First Arsch.” They came up with an album “Saddle Up” in 1992. In the 1990s, he worked as a lyricist. In 1994, the band won a competition in Berlin that permitted them to record an official demo of four tracks. During the early years of his band Rammstein, he extensively used “pyrotechnics,” and consequently burned his arms, hair, and ears. After his recovery, Till got certified in pyrotechnics. This allowed them to perform with pyrotechnics provided that they do it in a safe manner.

In 2002, he published his poetry book as Messer. It is a compilation of 54 poems by Gert Hof, who has been the author of the publication “Rammstein” and had been the pyro designer of the band for the past seven years.

In the Dresden gallery, Till presented his original writings of poems, along with the two sculptures in the book “In Silent Nights” in 2014.
