Why Did Florida Georgia Line Break Up?

Why Did Florida Georgia Line Break Up? Discover the reasons behind Florida Georgia Line's hiatus. Learn why Tyler Hubbard and Brian Kelley chose to explore solo careers, fostering personal growth while maintaining their friendship.

Florida Georgia Line

Florida Georgia Line, a renowned American country music duo, was founded in 2010 through the collaboration of two talented artists - Tyler Hubbard from Georgia and Brian Kelley from Florida. They burst onto the scene with their debut single "Cruise," which shattered records by becoming the first country song to achieve Diamond certification and dominating the digital country song sales charts.

Their unique musical style, often referred to as "bro-country," seamlessly blends elements of rock and hip-hop, while their lyrics touch on themes such as lively party scenes, truck culture, and romantic attraction.

Why Did Florida Georgia Line Break Up?

After a decade of making waves in the country music realm, Florida Georgia Line took an unexpected turn by announcing a hiatus. While they didn't officially dissolve the band, Tyler Hubbard and Brian Kelley decided to explore their individual artistic paths. This decision was fueled by a desire for creative growth and experimentation.

Importantly, both musicians emphasized that their parting was marked by mutual respect and there is no underlying animosity between them.

Florida Georgia Line History

The origin of Florida Georgia Line can be traced back to Nashville, Tennessee, where they started as a cover band. In December 2011, they signed with the Big Loud label, and their journey gained traction with the release of their second EP, "It'z Just What We Do," in 2012. This EP managed to secure a spot on the Billboard Top Country Albums chart, solidifying their presence in the industry.

The duo's transition to Republic Nashville, a division of Big Machine Records, marked a pivotal point in their career. Their subsequent albums, including "Anything Goes" (2014), "Dig Your Roots" (2016), "Can't Say I Ain't Country" (2019), and "Life Rolls On" (2021), demonstrated their consistency in delivering chart-topping country hits.

Tyler Hubbard and Brian Kelley Career

Tyler Hubbard and Brian Kelley, the driving forces behind Florida Georgia Line, have embarked on separate artistic journeys, reflecting their desire for growth and individual expression. Tyler Hubbard's solo foray has been marked by the release of successful singles like "5 Foot 9" and "Dancin' in the Country." His upcoming debut solo album promises to showcase his unique musical evolution.
