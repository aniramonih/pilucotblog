Honda Elevate vs Kia Seltos Compact SUV Comparison

Honda Elevate is challenging established players like the Kia Seltos in the SUV market. Let’s delve into an in-depth comparison between the two to ascertain which holds the edge. Let’s compare the new both mid size SUV! We’ll look at their features, sizes, and other cool features  to see which one is better. Join us to find out which car wins!

Honda Elevate cars prioritize passenger comfort. Premium materials, ample legroom, and adjustable seating can be expected. Also, features like a single-pane sunroof would enhance the cabin’s ambience. The Seltos offers ventilated front seats, a feature not commonly found in its segment. The cabin feels plush with leatherette upholstery and quality materials. The panoramic sunroof in the upcoming models will further enhance the luxurious feel.

Engine Performance and Specifications

Honda Elevate:

  • Engine: The Elevate is powered by Honda’s 1.5-litre i-VTEC petrol engine.
  • Output: This engine churns out 119bhp and 145Nm of torque.
  • Transmission Options: Available in a six-speed manual or a CVT.
  • Fuel Efficiency: It offers a claimed mileage of 15.31kmpl with the manual transmission and 16.92kmpl with the CVT.

Kia Seltos:

  • Engine: The Seltos is versatile, offering a 1.5-litre NA petrol, a 1.5-litre GDi turbo petrol, and a 1.5-litre turbo diesel engine.
  • Output: The NA petrol delivers a mileage of 17kmpl for MT and 17.7kmpl for CVT. The GDi turbo petrol offers 17.7kmpl for the iMT and 17.9kmpl for the DCT. The diesel variant returns 20.7kmpl for the MT and 19.1kmpl for the automatic transmission.
  • Transmission Options: Multiple options across engines – MT, CVT, iMT, DCT.

Features and Technology

Honda Elevate:

  • Honda, with the Elevate, has focused on modern integrations, such as the segment-first Lanewatch camera and the advanced Level-2 ADAS.
  • The Elevate also introduces a single-pane sunroof in its segment.

Kia Seltos:

  • Seltos is renowned for its tech-laden approach, coming with features like a 10.25-inch touchscreen infotainment system, UVO connected car technology, and a sound mood lighting system.
  • The Seltos also boasts a panoramic sunroof, contrasting Elevate’s single-pane version.

Final Thoughts:

Design is subjective, and while both the Honda Elevate and the Kia Seltos have their unique design cues, they cater to different tastes. The Elevate appears to target those looking for a sophisticated and elegant SUV, while the Seltos leans towards a younger, dynamic audience seeking a blend of luxury and functionality.

The Seltos, with its established reputation and extensive feature list, sets a high bar. However, Honda’s Elevate, with its fresh approach and Honda’s legacy, is not far behind.
