Career

Miller started his career with the bands like Elegants and the Frantics. He has also worked with Bobby Fuller Four and has gone on a tour with him and has also covered a guitar version of his song, “I Fought the Law.” He also released a song called “Spanish Castle Magic,” and he dedicated this song to his friends Jimi Hendrix and Larry Coryell. He met them while visiting Seattle; they would often meet in the club, Spanish Castle, hence the name of the song.

The group Moby Grape was founded in San Francisco in 1966 with band members, Jerry Miller, Don Stevenson, and Bob Mosley. The label Columbia signed the band. They made four albums under that label, which was released between 1967 and 1969. Some of their best works were “Hey Grandma,” “8.05”, “Murder In My Heart for The Judge.” The song “Hey Grandma” was used in the 2005 film “The Interpreter.” The disbandment took place in 1970, but they still performed occasionally on today’s date.

Jerry Miller and Don Stevenson later joined with John Barrett and John Oxendine to form the group The Rhythm Dukes in 1969. The group, however, was not a success, and it recorded only one album and disbanded in 1971.

After 1995, he returned to his hometown and formed The Jerry Miller Band with Tom Murphy, Darin Watkins, and Kim Workman. The band performed for the 40th Anniversary of Monterey Pop in July 2007. His latest album is “Miller Time.”
