Tom Bodett Accident: Where Is He Now? Injury And Health Update

Tom Bodett Accident news has worried fans. Here is the detailed information on his health update and his whereabouts. 

Tom Bodett is no stranger to the broadcasting industry. Having made his national debut on NPR’s evening news program All Things Considered in 1984, he is perhaps best known as the brand spokesman for the Motel 6 lodging chain.

Aside from his broadcasting career, Bodett is also a prolific author, having written seven books and numerous audio publications. His work has been featured in various national newspapers and periodicals, showcasing his versatility as a writer.

Furthermore, Tom has appeared on popular shows such as Saturday Night Live, National Geographic Explorer, and several Ken Burns documentaries.

In addition to his involvement in broadcasting and entertainment, Bodett is a regular panelist on NPR’s satirical weekend news quiz Wait, Wait…Don’t Tell Me and a performer for The Moth storytelling series.

Tom Bodett Accident And Injury 

In a fateful incident in Oregon, when Tom Bodett was just 20 years old, he experienced a life-altering accident. Trying to restore electricity to his cabin, he climbed a pole to reach the power line.

However, as he grabbed the lever, he was blown off the pole, suffering severe injuries. The accident practically blew his arm off and stopped his heart momentarily.

Miraculously, when he landed, he was revived, leaving medical professionals to believe that the impact of the fall revived him.

When asked how the accident changed him, Bodett reflected on his difficulty remembering the person he was before the incident.

Before the accident, he described himself as a cautious individual. However, the transformative experience of surviving the burns, enduring pain, and the trauma of the accident profoundly impacted him. It made him more fearless, seeking to explore life and all it had to offer.

Where Is Tom Bodett Now?

Following the accident, Bodett moved to Alaska, where he spent 23 years engaging in fishing, building homes, and developing his passion for writing.

He authored seven books, primarily centered around his adventurous experiences in Alaska. During this time, he gained fame for his Motel 6 commercials, which became well-known in the region.

During his time in Alaska, Bodett also struggled with alcoholism. However, in 1992, he decided to get sober and has been sober since then. 

Now at 68, Tom Bodett finds solace in spending the rest of his life working in his woodshop. He appreciates the advantage of having faced death once and cherishes the opportunity to continue pursuing his passion.

When humorously asked about having “We’ll leave the light on for you” on his tombstone, a famous line from his Motel 6 commercials, he jokingly expressed that he promised to haunt his loved ones if it were to appear, emphasizing the desire to be remembered for more than just his advertising work.

Tom Bodett Health Update

The renowned writer and radio essayist Tom seem to be in good health. Though specific details about his health may not be public, there haven’t been any reports or signs indicating any health problems.

Bodett is known for being lively and active, and he continues to enjoy various activities like woodworking and being involved with the HatchSpace non-profit center. 

His commitment to staying sober has positively impacted his overall well-being. Since 1992, he has embraced a sober lifestyle, which has given him a new lease on life.

Woodworking, in particular, has played a significant role in his recovery and happiness. It has become essential to his life, contributing to his overall well-being.

He keeps pursuing his passions, sharing his talents, and positively impacting the world around him.
