Early life

Born in Indiana, on November 30, 1965, Ryan Murphy is an American screenwriter, director, and former journalist. Ryan received his upbringing in an Irish Catholic family, and his family was very religious too. He did his early education in a Catholic School only. After the 8th grade, he moved to Warren Central High School and completed his high school. Later, he enrolled at Indiana University Bloomington and completed his graduation with a degree in journalism. Ryan followed his parent’s suit in his youth. His mother, J Andy Murphy, worked in the communication department while his father was a circulation director in the newspaper industry. Thus, Ryan too studied journalism but ended up becoming a screenwriter.

Speaking of his personal life, Ryan Murphy is gay. He has claimed it. In 2012, he married photographer, David Miller. The couple shares a good bonding and is faithful to each other. As of now, they’re the proud parents of two sons. Logan Phineas was born in 2012 while Ford was born in 2014.

Career

Ryan had an interest in glamour world since childhood. While in his schools, he was an essential member of his school’s theatre department and had also sung in the school choir. However, as he grew up, he chose to make his career in journalism, like his father. After completing his graduation, he worked as a journalist for nearly 7-8 years serving for Los Angeles Times, The Miami Herald, New York Daily News, and Entertainment Weekly. In the late 1990s, he realized that journalism was not for him, so he didn’t take long to change the gears.

He broke into scriptwriting, and Steven Spielberg purchased his first script. However, it earned him nothing more than a few bucks. His writing skills got noticed when he wrote the teen comedy series ‘Popular’. This TV series lasted for two years and gave him a scriptwriter status. In 2003, he wrote a drama series Nip/Truck which took him to a whole new level of popularity bagging him several award nominations and critical acclaim.

In 2009, he came up with another hit script, and it was Glee. He experimented with the drama-comedy and garnered tremendous accolades for his variations in writing. Following that, he wrote some other film screenplays and TV series. Lately, he also tried his hands in producing and directing and achieved a great success.

Awards & achievements

As a screenwriter, producer, and director, Ryan has won several awards and nominations. He has won 3 Primetime Emmy Awards, 1 BAFTA Award, 1 Critic Choice TV Award, 1 Hollywood Film Award, and Humanitas Prize. He also received a Grammy Award nomination.
