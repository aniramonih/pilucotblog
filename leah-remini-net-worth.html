FAQs about Leah Remini

Who is Leah Remini?

She is an American actress, author, and activist, best known for her role as Carrie Heffernan on The King of Queens. Beyond her acting career, she’s gained significant attention for her outspoken stance against the Church of Scientology, of which she was a member for over 30 years.

What is Remini known for?

Besides her acting, she is widely recognized for her critical view of Scientology, particularly through her Emmy-winning docuseries, Leah Remini: Scientology and the Aftermath, which exposes alleged abuses within the Church of Scientology.

Has Remini won any awards for her work?

Yes, Leah won an Emmy in 2017 for Outstanding Informational Series or Special for her series on Scientology.

What books has Remini written?

she authored Troublemaker: Surviving Hollywood and Scientology, a memoir detailing her experiences and eventual break with the Church of Scientology. The book is a New York Times Best Seller.

What TV shows has Remini appeared in?

Leah’s television career includes prominent roles in The King of Queens, Kevin Can Wait, and a reality TV show about her family called Leah Remini: It’s All Relative. She’s also made appearances on Friends, Saved by the Bell, and was a co-host on the first season of The Talk.

Did Remini participate in any reality TV shows?

Yes, she competed on Dancing with the Stars in season 17, finishing in fifth place. She also created and starred in her own reality TV series, Leah Remini: It’s All Relative.

What activism work does Remini do?

Leah’s activism is largely focused on exposing and criticizing the Church of Scientology. Through her documentary series, she has sought to bring to light the personal and financial tolls the Church exerts on its members.

What is Remini’s family background?

Leah was born and raised in Brooklyn, New York. She has an Italian father and an Austrian Jewish mother. She now lives in Los Angeles with her husband, Angelo, and daughter, Sofia.

When did Remini leave Scientology?

She left the Church of Scientology in 2013 after being a member since childhood, citing the church’s policies and the impact it had on her family relationships.

What recent projects is Remini involved with?

Leah continues to work in television and has expanded her role to include producing documentary programming. She’s also been a judge on the dance competition series So You Think You Can Dance as of 2022.
