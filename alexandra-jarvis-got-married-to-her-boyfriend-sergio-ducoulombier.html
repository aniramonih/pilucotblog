Alexandra Jarvis Got Married To Her Boyfriend Sergio Ducoulombier

Alexandra Jarvis married Sergio Ducoulombier on April 14, 2023, in Lake Como, Italy, at the Villa del Balbianello. Alexandra Jarvis appeared on Season 1 of Selling the OC.

She joined the show after graduating from Auburn University as an associate at Oppenheim Group. She made around $40 million in sales in her first year in the industry. She’s also appeared in films like Clock Work and Feedback. On June 15, 2019, Sergio Ducoulombier, the founder and CEO of payment network Slip Cash Inc., met her at a restaurant.

According to Sergio Ducoulombier’s LinkedIn page, the goal of Slip and Cash is to “enable frictionless cashless acceptance for anything, anywhere, and at any time from anyone’s phone for any reason.” Sergio Ducoulombier earned a Bachelor of Business

Administration and Management degree from the University of Washington in 1999. There is no more information available on Ducoulombier on the Internet, but he began working at Slip and Cash in 2019.

Alexandra Jarvis expressed a desire for an unconventional wedding

They exchanged phone numbers when they met for cocktails at the restaurant. On December 27, 2020, they were engaged in a private ceremony in Newport Beach, California. The couple’s Instagram account was then updated with the news. Alexandra Jarvis, star of Selling the OC, captioned a photo of them kissing:

“A fantastic way to bring in the New Year.” I’m ecstatic to marry the love of my life, my best friend, and ride or die.”

The wedding will take place on April 14, 2023. In an interview with People, Alexandra Jarvis stated the following about her wedding:

“We wanted a very intimate and romantic wedding day that was free of all of the traditional expectations.” So we decided to go to Italy by ourselves. We’ve been there before, and I told him I loved him there. He had previously told me that he loved me, but I had resisted for a long time.”

Furthermore, Jarvis stated:

“It’s a special spot for us.” We chose Lake Como for our wedding because the scenery and natural beauty are breathtaking. It’s the perfect, romantic backdrop for what we had in mind.”

During the interview, Alexandra Jarvis also stated that she did not want a traditional wedding and the burden of entertaining everyone who would attend. She desired a small wedding so that the couple could exchange vows and enjoy their time together.

Michael Costello, a well-known designer, created Alexandra Jarvis’ wedding gown

During the interview, she explained how she got involved in the design of the dress:

“It’s beautiful. It’s a completely unique gown. I met with Michael months ago, and we sat down, and I basically described my concept to him, and he drew it out. That’s exactly what I had in mind. He has a lot of talent.”

Sergio Ducoulombier and Alexandra took a private boat ride to the nearby town of Bellagio shortly after exchanging vows. The evening came to a close with an Italian dinner. Sergio Ducoulombier and Alexandra returned to Orange County after a wedding celebration with family and friends.
