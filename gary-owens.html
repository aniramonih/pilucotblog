Gary Owens was an American radio personality, announcer, disc jockey, voice actor, and actor who was best known for being the announcer on Rowan & Martin’s Laugh-In, a sketch comedy television show. In addition to that, he also showcased his acting skills in films and shows such as Sesame Street, Barnaby Jones, Return from Witch Mountain, Simon & Simon, Sledge Hammer!, Night Court, Sabrina, the Teenage Witch, Dynomutt, Dog Wonder, Square One Television, The Super Mario Bros. Super Show!, Bobby’s World, 2 Stupid Dogs, and The New Batman Adventures.

    Gary Owens Facts

  • He was 8 years old when he was diagnosed with Type 1 diabetes.
  • His book, The (What to Do While You’re Holding the) Phone Book, was published in 1973.
  • He served as the honorary sheriff of Encino, California.
  • His trademark self-introduction was “This is Gary Owens, friend of those who want no friends, going places and losing things,” or occasionally, “Hello, and also hi; but not necessarily in that order”.
  • In 1980, he received a Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame, between those of Walt Disney and Betty White, at 6743 Hollywood Boulevard in Hollywood, California.
  • Gary Owens also played an important role in helping the Stooges get a Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame and organized a campaign in 1983 for their fans and it received 15 thousand letters from fans who wanted it.
  • He did voiceover work for video games like Space Quest IV: Roger Wilco and the Time Rippers, Space Quest 6: The Spinal Frontier, and Nickelodeon 3D Movie Maker.
  • In 1994, he was inducted into the National Radio Hall of Fame.
  • Owens coined the phrase “Beautiful downtown Burbank”.
