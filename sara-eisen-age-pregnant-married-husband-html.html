Sara Eisen Deceiving Age Married Journalist With Editor Husband &amp; Baby

On first glance, Sara Eisen looks like someone in her twenties, who has just graduated from college. However, behind the beautiful smiley face is an unbelievable number and a happy family. 

Sara Eisen's presenting career got going after starting with Bloomberg. However, the 33-year-olds work came to fore after joining with CNBC, where she hosts the 90-minute business program Squawk on the Street, and also appears as a reporter for other programs. 

CNBC Television: Sara Eisen reports about Under Armour prohibition of expensing strip club visits (Published on 6 November 2018)

Sara Eisen is very active on social media, Twitter with more than 85 thousand followers.

Sara Was Born To Doctor Parents

Born to parents; father Drore Eisen, a dermatologist, as well as founder and director of CDx Diagnostics and mother Jane Eisen, a former pediatric dentist in the early 1970’s, Sara never got inclined to their influence.

As being fascinated by journalism from an early age, she attended the New York University for her graduation. Later, for her masters, she chose broadcast journalism in business reporting as her primary and post graduated from the Medill School of Journalism.

Post graduation, she got the opportunity to attend a Jewish summer camp, called “Camp Ramah,” which also included notable personalities; CNN’s Jake Tapper and Wolf Blitzer, the former US Federal Reserve chairman Ben Bernanke, and the businessman Michael Dell perhaps giving Sara career options?

Professional Life; She Joined CNBC In 2013

After finishing up her degree, she joined up with Bloomberg in 2010. During her stay with the news network, she co-anchored the Bloomberg Surveillance, along with reporting for the Bloomberg news. She specialized in global macroeconomics, consumer policy, and business with the Bloomberg along with covering up for the network in events such as Fukushima disaster, European monetary crisis and the aftermath of Tsunami. 

She made a high profile switch in her career when she decided to join up with the CNBC in 2013. Announcing in her Twitter in November 2013, she thanked up the network and could not hide her excitement beginning up a new chapter in her professional life. 

Initially joining up as a correspondent for CNBC specializing in global consumer habits, she quickly rose up ranks and became a co-host for Worldwide Exchange. After that, she hosted the CNBC's flagship program live from the New York Exchange, Squawk on the Street from 2014 and was appointed as the co-host of CNBC's Power Lunch.

Despite all the hassles associated with the job which includes odd and long working shifts along with constant traveling, her face shows no signs of strains.

Married A Co-Worker And Already Pregnant With A Son; Still Looks Stunning

Sara tied the knots with her boyfriend and longtime co-worker Matthew Stone Levine on 29 May 2016. Sara met with Mathew, managing editor of Bloomberg Television, during her Bloomberg days in 2011 and have been together ever since. 

Sara got pregnant about a year on from her marriage. Sara posted a picture where she was seen holding her son on mother's day 2018 and wrote that she had found a new respect for all the mothers after having her baby.
