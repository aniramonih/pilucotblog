William Windom Biography

William Windom is one of the most popular and richest TV Actor who was born on September 28, 1923 in New York, New York, United States. Perhaps most well known for his Emmy-winning portrayal of John Monroe in the comedy series My World and Welcome to It, Windom is also remembered for his recurring role as Dr. Seth Hazlitt on CBS’s Murder, She Wrote. He also made a memorable appearance in the 1967 Star Trek episode “The Doomsday Machine.”

Actress Inger Stevens played Windom’s character’s (Glen Morley’s) daughter on the ABC situation comedy The Farmer’s Daughter.

Born in New York City, he was the son of Isobel Peckham and architect Paul Windom and the great-grandson of U.S. Republican Party politician and Secretary of the Treasury William Windom . His five marriages – to Carol Keyser, Barbara Joyce, Barbara Goetz, Jacqulyn Hopkins, and Patricia Tunder – produced a total of six children.
