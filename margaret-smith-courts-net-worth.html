Margaret Smith Court Net Worth: Lifestyle &amp; Career

Margaret Smith Court is a retired Australian tennis player with a net worth of $10 million as of December 2023.

She is known for her successful tennis career and as the first female Australian tennis player to dominate the Australian, French, and U.S. Tennis Grand Slam.

Also, she is one of the only three players in history to have won the “Grand Slam Boxed Set,” consisting of every Grand Slam title (Singles, Doubles, and Mixed Doubles).

Similarly, she is the only player in history to complete a multiple Grand Slam Set twice in all three disciplines, i.e., Singles, Women’s Doubles, and Mixed Doubles.

In 2010, the Herald Sun named Court the Greatest Female Tennis Player of All Time.

Margaret Smith Court | Ministry & Controversy

Ministry

Mrs. Court is a person known for her belief in God and was raised a Roman Catholic; she was later involved with Pentecostalism in the mid-’70s.

Court gained her theological qualification from the Rhema Bible Training Centre.

In 1991, she was ordained as an independent Pentecostal minister. Later on, she founded a church known as the Victoria Life Centre.

Likewise, she has been seen primarily conveying the message about her faith to the public as she still serves as the senior pastor of Victoria Life Centre.

She has embraced teachings associated with the world of faith, which is why she prefers to stay subtle.

Controversy

As a religious minister, Mrs. Court teaches her view of biblical doctrine but has been a constant citric of L.G.B.T.Q. Rights and same-sex marriage in Australia.

Further, in 2017, Court criticized Qantas for supporting same-sex marriage by writing a letter to The West Australian daily paper. This led to the rise of criticism against her views.

Criticism of Qantas drove calls for the Margaret Court Field to be renamed, but a few politicians, namely Prime Serve Malcolm Turnbull, rejected the thought.

In January 2019, Anna Wintour renewed calls to rename the remaining Margaret Court’s Arena. Court responded by saying she was disappointed that “someone who came from America” could not tolerate her views.

The controversy led high-profile guests Martina Navratilova and John McEnroe to hold a parade that called for the Margaret Court Arena to be renamed in honor of the four-time Australian Open champion Evonne Goolagong.

Margaret Smith Court | Career

Mrs. Margaret Smith Court’s career began when she was 8 years old as she used to play in the middle of the road using discarded green-stained balls from under the tennis court fence across her house.

She was an ordinary left-handed player who had been influenced to alter to a right-handed grasp.

At 18, she won the first of seven successive singles titles at the Australian Championships in 1960.

Eventually, she became the first female player from Australia to win a Grand Slam tournament overseas.

She won the French and U.S. Championships in 1962 and also became the first Australian woman to win Wimbledon across Singles, Doubles, and Mixed Doubles.

Margaret won a surprising 64 Grand Slams.

After the Wimbledon match, she took a gap from tennis as she got married to Barry Court in 1967.

Later, she returned to tennis to win all four Grand Slam Singles titles in 1970.

In January 2003, one of the sports and entertainment complexes at Melbourne Park was renamed Margaret Court Arena as a tribute to her sportsmanship.
