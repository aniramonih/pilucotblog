Jordan Henderson is a professional English footballer who started his career with the professional football club Sunderland in 1998 in their youth team and has prospered enough to become the captain of Liverpool’s Premier League. He has also lead the English national team consecutively in 2014 and 2018 FIFA World Cups. Playing as a midfielder, Henderson has become one of the most influential players for Liverpool.

    • In November 2008, Jordan made his professional debut in Sunderland AFC‘s league match against Chelsea. He was sent in as a substitute at the half-time in his side’s 5-0 mauling.
    • In August 2011, he made his first competitive start for Liverpool in a league match against his former side Sunderland AFC. The match ended as a 1-1 draw and he was booed by some sections of Sunderland’s fans.
    • In November 2010, he made his first appearance for the England national team in a friendly match against France. He was paired with his club captain Steven Gerrard in the center of the park.

    Jordan Henderson Facts

  • The 2009-10 season proved to be his breakthrough season as he became a regular fixture in the Sunderland first team.
  • Although he has cemented his reputation as a good central midfielder, he started his professional career as a right midfield player. He first got an opportunity to play centrally for Sunderland senior team after Lee Cattermole got injured.
  • At the end of 2009-10 season, he was selected as Sunderland’s Young Player of the Year. He won the award at the end of the next season as well.
  • In June 2011, Jordan moved to Liverpool as their manager and club legend Kenny Daglish reportedly convinced his club to pay around £16 million to Sunderland for Henderson.
  • At the end of his debut season with Liverpool, he was honored with Liverpool Young Player of the Year Award.
  • At the start of 2012-13 season, the new Liverpool manager Brendan Rodgers told him that he could move to Fulham if he wanted because he wasn’t in his plans. However, Henderson rejected the move.
  • In April 2015, he signed a new five year deal with Liverpool that made him one of the best players at the club with a weekly wage of £100,000.
  • In 2016, he was featured alongside Lionel Messi on the cover of the FIFA 16’s UK edition.
