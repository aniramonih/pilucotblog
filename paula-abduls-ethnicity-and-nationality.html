What Is Paula Abdul’s Nationality? 

Paula Abdul is a bonafide American woman. The singer was born in San Fernando, a general-law city in the San Fernando Valley region of Los Angeles County, California, USA. This was also where she was raised. She was born to a Jewish family and raised in the Jewish tradition.

Paula is not an only child; she has an older sister who is called Wendy. It is reported that both Paula and her sister, Wendy, are very close. The sisters spent lots of time together while growing up in San Fernando.

Paula Abdul is of Mixed Ethnicity

Even though Paula Abdul was born in the USA and is a bonafide American woman, we have found out that the singer is actually of mixed ethnicity. As we have mentioned before, she was born into a Jewish family because both her parents are Jewish.

According to our findings, Paula Abdul is actually of Syrian Jewish heritage, and she also has Canadian roots. Paula is very proud of her Jewish heritage and often talks about it whenever she gets the chance to do so. In fact, for years, she publicly explained that her dream is to go to Israel for a real holiday and connect with her roots.

Eventually, in November 2006, the Israeli Tourism Minister Isaac Herzog invited her to Israel, and this got Paula Abdul really excited. She immediately responded by hugging the minister and telling him excitedly that she would come. She also explained that the man had helped her make a dream come true. In 2013, Paula finally fulfilled her dreams and got to visit Israel, where she connected with her Jewish roots. She described the visit as ‘the most magnificent trip ever.’

Also, in 2013, when she was the age of 51, Paula had her bat mitzvah, a Jewish coming-of-age ritual, in Safed, Israel. She did this at the International Center for Tzfat Kabbalah, a museum and spiritual retreat for the study of Kabbalah – Jewish mysticism. Essentially, Paula Abdul is very proud of her roots as a Jewish woman and tries to connect with her roots as often as she can.

Paula Abdul is also very proud of her Canadian roots. In 2012, the actress stormed Toronto, the capital of the province of Ontario, Canada. While there, she connected with her people and talked extensively about her Canadian roots, explaining how proud she is to be from there.

Where Did Paula Abdul Grow Up?

Paula Abdul grew up in her town of birth – San Fernando, a general-law city in the San Fernando Valley region of Los Angeles County, California. She was raised in San Fernando alongside her sister, Wendy.

Growing up in San Fernando, she watched her mother, who was a concert pianist, play the piano and quickly took interest in music. She then began taking dance lessons at an early age in ballet, jazz, and tap. Paula later attended Van Nuys High School and became a cheerleader and an honor student there.

When she was 15 years old, she received a scholarship to a dance camp near Palm Springs, a development that truly excited her. Years later, in 1978, she appeared in a low-budget independent musical film called Junior High School. After graduating from Van Nuys High School In 1980,  Paula went on to study broadcasting at California State University, Northridge.

While still in her freshman year, Paula Abdul got selected from a pool of 700 candidates for the cheerleading squad of the Los Angeles Lakers NBA basketball team. She did so well that in the space of just one year, she had grown to become the head choreographer. Paula Abdul was later discovered by The Jacksons, the world-famous American pop band composed of members of the legendary Jackson family, including Michael Jackson.

She was discovered after a few of the band members had watched her while attending a Los Angeles Lakers game. After she got discovered, Paula was eventually signed to do the choreography for the video to the group’s single “Torture”. From there, she went on to build her own career and became a very successful music star who is known around the world.
