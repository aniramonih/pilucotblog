Who is Beanie Feldstein?

Elizabeth Greer “Beanie” Feldstein is an American actress.

Her breakthrough roles were in the comedies Neighbours 2: Sorority Rising (2016), Lady Bird (2017), and Booksmart (2019), the latter of which earned her a Golden Globe nomination for Best Actress – Motion Picture Comedy or Musical.

She later rose to prominence for her supporting turns in the dramas Lady Bird (2017) and Lady Bird (2018).

Feldstein has performed on the Broadway stage, making her stage debut as Minnie Fay in Hello, Dolly! (2017–18) and playing the title role of Fanny Brice in the first revival of Funny Girl (2022), for which she received a Drama League Award nomination for Distinguished Performance.

She played Monica Lewinsky in the third season of American Crime Story: Impeachment on FX in 2021.

Early Life of Beanie Feldstein

Beanie Feldstein was born on June 29, 1993, in Los Angeles, USA. She was raised and brought up in America.

The lone child of Guns N’ Roses tour accountant Richard Feldstein and Sharon Lyn (née Chalkin), a costume designer and fashion stylist. Feldstein is a Jew.

The youngest of three children, she is. Her eldest brother, Maroon 5 manager Jordan Feldstein (1977-2017), died suddenly at age 40 from a blood clot; her elder brother is actor Jonah Hill.

When she was a baby, her nanny gave her the nickname “Beanie,” and her brothers followed suit. She went to Stagedoor Manor, a theatre camp in New York, as well as the Harvard-Westlake School in Los Angeles.

Moreover, she has been best friends with Ben Platt, a Broadway and film actor, since high school.

She earned a sociology degree from Wesleyan University in 2015.

Beanie Feldstein Career

In the ABC comedy series My Wife and Kids in 2002, Feldstein made her acting debut. She portrayed Megan in the musical Madison High television pilot in 2012.

In the third season of the Netflix comedy Orange Is the New Black in 2015, Feldstein made an appearance as a guest star. In the comedy Fan Girl that same year, she portrayed Anna.

Feldstein gained recognition for the larger part he played alongside Seth Rogen and Zac Efron in the comedy Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising.

The movie was made available on May 20, 2016.

The role of Lydia Harris in the HBO period drama The Devil You Know was cast as Lydia Harris by Feldstein. In an episode of the NBC sitcom Will & Grace, she appeared as a guest star.

It was announced on October 18, 2016, that Feldstein would play Minnie Fay in the 2017 Broadway production of Hello, Dolly! with Bette Midler.

In this role, Feldstein made her Broadway debut. The performance debuted on April 20, 2017, after the start of previews on March 15. The critics praised her for her performance.

The Female Brain, a comedy directed by Whitney Cummings, starred Feldstein and had its world premiere in June 2017 at the LA Film Festival.

Saoirse Ronan, Laurie Metcalf, and she co-starred in Greta Gerwig’s 2017 critically acclaimed solo directorial debut, Lady Bird.

The movie received an Academy Award nomination for Best Picture. She was nominated for the Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance with the rest of the cast.

More about her career

In the first season of the television adaptation of the horror comedy movie What We Do in the Shadows, which debuted in 2019, Feldstein played the character Jenna on a regular basis.

The critics praised her for her performance. Due to other filming commitments, she was unable to make her anticipated return for the second season.

She also appeared in Olivia Wilde’s high school comedy Booksmart, which she directed for the first time in 2019.

Later, she played Johanna Morrigan, a British lead character, in the coming-of-age comedy How to Build a Girl, which was based on the 2014 book by Caitlin Moran.

She made a guest appearance in the ABC medical drama series Grey’s Anatomy in 2020 as Tess Anderson.

Since she was a young girl, Feldstein said, appearing in the series had been her dream.

She provided voice work for the character of a support group therapist on The Simpsons in the episode “Frinkcoin.”

She participated in the television specials Saturday Night Seder and Take Me to the World: A Sondheim 90th Celebration. On the latter program, she and Ben Platt sang a cover of “It Takes Two” from Into the Woods.

Based on Stephen Karam’s one-act play of the same name, Feldstein played the lead role in the drama film The Humans.
