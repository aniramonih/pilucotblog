Netflix Ginny & Georgia Actor Antonia Gentry Bio & Wiki

Antonia Bonea Gentry, aka Antonia Gentry (born on September 25, 1997), is a 25-year-old American actress who is known for her work in the TV industry. Toni entered the acting industry in 2014, playing the role of a Teenage Driver in “PSA Don’t Text and Drive Pay Attention.” Just a year after its debut, Gentry starred as Sara in the film named “Driver’s Ed: Tales from the Street.”

In 2021, Antonia gathered accolades for her performance in “Ginny & Georgia.” Here she played the role of a 15-year-old girl named Ginny Miller, who often feels that she is more mature than her 30-year-old mother, Georgia Miller (Brianne Howey). The audience loved the show’s storyline, which motivated makers to come up with the second installment, which aired on January 5, 2023.

According to Toni’s IMDB profile, she is working with Hannah Macpherson on a horror movie titled “Time Cut.” The release date of Gentry’s upcoming film has yet to be out, but there are speculations that we will soon get to watch it on Netflix.

How Old is Antonia Gentry?

On September 25, 1997, Antonia Bonea Gentry (age 25) was born in Atlanta, Georgia, America. Sources revealed that Toni’s father is caucasian, whereas her mother is Jamaican. She was only 5 when the dream to become an actress germinated in her mind. Antonia’s mom, who wrote plays for a community theatre, helped her learn theatre.

In 2015 she completed her schooling at ohn S. Davidson Fine Arts Magnet School and enrolled at Emory University for higher studies. In her first year of college, she gave multiple auditions and fortunately managed to land the role of Sara in “Driver’s Ed: Tales from the Street.” It was a comedy movie where Barbara Zagrodnik filmed Gentry alongside artists like Karlton T. Clay, Justin Wheelon, Chris Shurley, and Kristy White.

Antonia Gentry’s Acting Career

After a decent debut, Antonia decided to work on her skills first and then look for acting opportunities. So she first completed her college studies, gained requisite knowledge, and then returned to the acting business with “Candy Jar,” a Ben Shelton-directed comedy movie featuring Jacob Latimore and Sami Gayle, in lead and co-lead, respectively. Other stars who were part of this Netflix production were Tom Bergeron, Helen Hunt, Paul Tigue, and Tom Bergeron.

Just a year later, this Atlanta-born actress worked with Carol Barbee on a sci-fi drama, “Raising Dion,” playing Wendy. Most of the parts that Antonia got picked for were either uncredited or minor, which is why her career was not taking off.

Finally, in 2020, Gentry received a call from her manager, who informed her about her selection for the lead character Ginny Miller in “Ginny & Georgia.” The young actress was so shocked that she could not believe that Jason Knight got so impressed with her audition that he offered her the lead role.
