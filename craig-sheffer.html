    Craig Sheffer Career

    Sheffer was first seen on a national origin as Ian Hayden on the ABC daytime soap opera One Life to Live and on the 1983 primetime seriesThe Hamptons. His most earlier starring role in films was as the best friend of troubled teenager Emilio Estevez in That Was Then… This Is Now (1985), a reformatory camp inmate in Fire with Fire (1986), an aggressive rich kid in Some Kind of Wonderful (1987), and the romantic love interest of an overweight cosmetician in Baby Cakes (1989).

    Sheffer played the main role of Aaron Boone/Cabal in Nightbreed (1990), author Norman Maclean in A River Runs Through It (1992), and played Heisman Trophy candidate quarterback Joe Kane in The Program in 1993.

    Sheffer’s additional films include Fire in the Sky (1993), Head Above Water (1996), and Sleep With Me (1994) (top-billed, as the apex of a romantic triangle that included Eric Stoltz and Meg Tilly). Sheffer played the major role in Clive Barker’s franchise sequel, Hellraiser: Inferno. Additionally, he also played the leading character in the Movie Bliss which starred Leigh Taylor-Young, Terence Stamp, and Sheryl Lee.

    Sheffer played Constant Bradley in the 1996 miniseries A Season in Purgatory and Keith Scott on the CW Network series One Tree Hill. Sheffer was a regular for three seasons; his character was killed midway through season 3. He had several minor appearances in season 4. He directed the feature film American Crude, a dark comedy starring Ron Livingston and Rob Schneider; it was released straight to DVD in June 2008. Sheffer had a cameo appearance in the movie Stand Up Guys. In 2016, Sheffer starred alongside Steven Seagal in Code of Honor.
