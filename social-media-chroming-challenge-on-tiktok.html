What Is Social Media Chroming Challenge On TikTok?

Before we dive into the tragic story of Esra Haynes, let’s first understand what chroming is and how it works.

Chroming, also called huffing or sniffing, is the act of inhaling the fumes of harmful substances to get high.

These substances can include paint, petrol, glue, deodorant, or other aerosol cans that are easy to access and cheap to buy.

Some people may also use nitrous gas or whipped cream chargers, which are also known as nangs1.

When these substances are inhaled, they enter the bloodstream through the lungs and reach the brain very quickly.

They act as depressant drugs, which means they slow down the activity of the central nervous system and affect the way the brain processes information.

This can cause a range of effects, such as euphoria, relaxation, drowsiness, hallucinations, or altered perception of time and space.

Some people may chrome because they are curious, bored, stressed, or pressured by their peers.

They may also have underlying mental health issues or trauma that make them seek an escape from reality.

However, chroming is not a harmless or fun activity.

It can have serious and even fatal consequences for your health and well-being.
