Career

Nicolas Jackson started his professional career at Casa Sports, a team in the Senegalese Premier League. In September 2019, he transferred to Villarreal and was subsequently loaned to Mirandes, a second-tier Spanish team. At Mirandes, he played in 20 league matches and scored two goals.

After returning to Villarreal in the summer of 2020, Jackson joined the club’s B-team in Primera División RFEF. Throughout the season, he proved his worth by scoring an impressive seven goals in 27 league matches, aiding the team in their promotion to the second division. Finally, on October 3, 2021, Jackson made his long-awaited debut for the first team, substituting Arnaut Danjuma and contributing to a triumphant 2-0 home victory over Real Betis.

Nicolas Jackson achieved his inaugural La Liga goal on August 13, 2022, securing the opening point in a triumphant 3-0 away victory against Real Valladolid. After thirteen days, Jackson and his teammate Álex Baena were elevated to the primary squad.

Jackson officially joined Chelsea in the Premier League on June 30, 2023, securing a lucrative eight-year contract until 2031 for a transfer fee of approximately £32 million. His first appearance for the club occurred on August 13, 2023, during a match against Liverpool, which resulted in a 1-1 draw. Jackson then showcased his scoring abilities on August 25, 2023, by netting his inaugural goal for Chelsea in a commanding 3-0 victory against newly promoted team Luton Town at Stamford Bridge.

On November 6, 2023, in a highly anticipated match between Tottenham and Chelsea, the Blues emerged victorious with a dominant 4-1 scoreline. This remarkable victory was largely owed to the outstanding performance of Nicolas Jackson, who managed to net three goals and complete a hat-trick. 

Despite facing a heavily determined Spurs side that was reduced to nine players during the game, Mauricio Pochettino‘s return to north London proved tumultuous as his team succumbed to defeat against their rivals. The win for Chelsea was hard-fought, showcasing their remarkable resilience and determination on the football pitch.
