Early Life & Biography

Born on 14 October 1946 to parents Elizabeth and John Venter in Salt Lake City, Utah, the U.S. He graduated high school in Millbrae, California, where he lived. He served in a field hospital’s intensive care ward in the United States Navy. When he was in Vietnam, he attempted suicide by swimming off to sea but changed his mind after swimming a mile out. His desire and belief in medicine emerged while faced with severe injuries and dying marines, while he later branched into biomedical science. He went to California’s San Mateo College but made a move to California University.

Career

Venter had been a crucial figure in some of the most astounding projects in the new science of genomics. The scientific team which worked under him was the first most to interpret the genome of a living organism and played the essential role in the formation of the first complete map of the human genome.

Venter’s expedition GLOBAL OCEAN SAMPLING EXPEDITION uncovered more genes that have ever been documented before. His team originated the world’s first synthetic species of bacteria after they raised a completely new DNA molecule in the lab. He had been declared as one of the most influential figures in the world in the TIME AND NEW STATESMAN and was awarded THE FINANCIAL TIMES MAN OF 2000.
