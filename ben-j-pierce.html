Ben J. Pierce is an American YouTube star, actor, singer, songwriter, editor, social media personality, dancer, and a self-proclaimed eyebrow artist who is known for uploading loads of interesting videos on her YouTube channels. The videos basically comprise of makeup tutorials, collaborations, transformation videos, comic skits, music videos, and her daily life and have made her a very popular internet personality. After being a closeted teenager, Ben J. Pierce finally came out as gay and queer on June 6, 2017, and has since been a major supporter of the LGBT community. She has also been cast in a variety of shows and movies such as Esther: Never Again, Hiding in Plain Sight, The Notebook Map, Slash, Guidance, Searching, Reverie, Fuller House, Zall Good, and BrainSurge. Additionally, Ben J. Pierce has branched out as a singer and has released several tracks such as Little Game, Boys Will Be Boys, and Rendezvous. She also enjoys a huge social media fan base and has garnered more than 250k followers on Instagram, more than 250k subscribers and more than 6.5 million views on her YouTube channel titled Ben J. Pierce, more than 300k subscribers and more than 25 million views on her YouTube channel titled BENNY, more than 10k followers on Facebook, and more than 120k followers on Twitter.

As a Singer

Ben J. Pierce released her first single, Little Game, in 2014. Then, in 2016, she released her another single, titled Boys Will Be Boys, which addressed toxic masculinity and heteronormativity and whose video featured actor Kalama Epstein.

Over the years, she has released a couple of other singles such as Never Apart, Rendezvous, and Every Boy.

First Film

Ben J. Pierce made her first feature film appearance by playing the role of Chayyim Rendenstein in the historical drama film, Esther: Never Again, in 2012. Her co-stars in the film included the likes of Cody Berry, Brett Herbert, Emylee Herring, Jeff Kelly, and Christopher Morris McGilvray.

The same year i.e. in 2012, Ben J. Pierce made her first theatrical film appearance as a student in the drama film, Hiding in Plain Sight. It starred Kelly O’Neil Jackson, Sharice Henry Chasi, Irma P. Hall, Drew Waters, Martin Ezelle, Gwendolynn Murphy, and Aaliyah Muhammad.

First TV Show

She made her first TV show appearance as a contestant on BrainSurge in 2011.

First Web Show

Ben J. Pierce made her first web show appearance as Lionel Gray in an episode, titled Ultimate Sports Fan, of AwesomenessTV in November 2014.

Ben J. Pierce Facts

  • Her pronouns are “she/her”.
  • She grew up in Dallas, Texas alongside her older siblings who often help her in her videos.
  • Ben J. Pierce’s nickname ‘Miss Benny’ beautifully amalgamates a masculine and yet feminine image.
  • Growing up, she was a closeted teenager and came out as gay on June 6, 2017, by uploading a video titled “Sincerely, an internet gay boy – (coming out)”.
  • At the age of 11, she started her YouTube journey by uploading videos on several channels. This helped her feel less isolated and lonely.
  • In 2012, Ben J. Pierce started uploading videos on her YouTube channel titled KidPOV which she later renamed to her own name in 2016.
  • She has collaborated with artists such as Dodie Clark, Jon Cozart, Tyler Oakley, Thomas Sanders, and James Charles.
  • Ben J. Pierce is friends with Australian singer, songwriter, actor, and YouTuber, Troye Sivan, and YouTuber, activist, and author, Tyler Oakley.
  • In 2011, she was cast in the Nickelodeon game show, BrainSurge, alongside Jordin Sparks, and wrapped up her journey successfully emerging as a winner.
  • As an editor, Ben J. Pierce has worked on projects such as Transforming Thomas Sanders Into: A Disney Villain (2016) and Tomorrow Never Came ft. Thomas Sanders (2017).
  • She is a very talented makeup artist and transformed Tyler Oakley to look just like her in one of her YouTube videos, titled TRANSFORMING TYLER OAKLEY INTO ME, in June 2015.
  • One of her aesthetic influencers is the photographer and filmmaker, Bob Mizer.
  • In 2016, Ben J. Pierce played the role of Smiley in the teen drama web series, Guidance.
  • She loves listening to hip hop and rap music and enjoys the work by artists such as Charli XCX and Carly Rae Jepsen.
