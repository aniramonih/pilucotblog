Mr. Probz Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Career Beginnings

Born Dennis Princewell Stehr, Mr. Probz grew up in Zoetermeer, Netherlands, and began his career not in music but as a graffiti artist.

His creative expression soon transitioned into music, capturing the complex rhythms of his daily life and translating them into lyrics. This early blend of art and music set the stage for a versatile career ahead.

Breakthrough with Waves

He first caught the public’s attention with the soulful guitar ballad Waves in 2013. This track not only showcased his melodic prowess but also his depth as a lyricist.

The subsequent remix by Robin Schulz propelled him onto the international stage, hitting No. 1 in over 51 countries and securing a Grammy nomination.

This single became a cornerstone of his career, leading to one billion streams on Spotify, marking him as the first Dutch artist to reach such a milestone.

Ventures in Music Production

As the founder of Left Lane Recordings, he has been deeply involved in various aspects of the music industry. This includes writing, producing, and releasing music both in Dutch and English.

His label has been instrumental in nurturing talent and contributing to the music scene, underscoring his role not just as an artist but as a mentor and business leader in the industry.

Collaborations and Achievements

Over the years, he has collaborated with a host of notable artists like Chris Brown, 50 Cent, and Anderson .Paak, among others. Each partnership has enhanced his profile and broadened his musical horizon.

Additionally, his song Nothing Really Matters reached the top of the charts, and he has been a pivotal part of Armin van Buuren’s world tour, further solidifying his position in music lore.

Overcoming Adversities

Life has not been without its challenges for Mr. Probz. He survived a shooting in 2010 and a devastating fire in 2013 that destroyed his studio.

Despite these setbacks, he released his album The Treatment for free as a thank you to his fans, showcasing his resilience and gratitude.

His legal battles with Sony Music over royalty disputes also depict a man not afraid to stand up for his rights, ultimately regaining control over his music.

Philanthropy and Beyond

Aside from music and legal battles, he extends his influence into real estate and philanthropy, showing a keen acumen for business and a heart for giving back.

His entrepreneurial ventures complement his artistic endeavors, creating a holistic image of an artist who is not only about beats and lyrics but also about impact and integrity.

His life and career have been so impactful that they were documented in Against the Stream, a documentary that aired on Red Bull TV and RTL5, highlighting his trials and triumphs.
