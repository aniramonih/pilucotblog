Who is John Mulaney?His wife Annamarie Tendler, net worth and other facts

John Mulaney is a funny man. One of the few special people on this earth who managed to make a living as a comedian. He knew from a young age that he wanted to entertain himself, and every step he took propelled him to the fame and fortune found in comedy. To find out more about the comedian who has made you laugh in the past, read on.

John Mulaney Biography

John Mulaney was born on August 26, 1982in the city of Chicago in Illinois. His parents, a law professor named Ellen Stanton and a lawyer named Charles W. Mulaney, were of Irish descent. Raised in a Catholic home with his three siblings, he watched Ricky Ricardo from a young age on I love Lucy and fall in love with show business. John later got the opportunity to audition for the character of Kevin in the hilarious Home Alone movie when he was only 7 years old. However, his parents were skeptical and refused.

Mulaney therefore had a more traditional St. Clement School education for junior high. Still, the aspiring comedian found a way to make school work for him. Instead of reports, he would meet up with his best friend named John O’Brien, and the two would do skits about what they had learned at school. He was also a frequent visitor to the Museum of Broadcast Communications, which allowed him to watch archived episodes of the shows he loved.

The aspiring comedian graduated from St. Ignatius College Prep in 2000 and attended the university his parents attended – Georgetown University – to major in English literature and religion. In Georgetown he joined an improv group that included Nick Kroll and Mike Birbiglia. After they bonded with the group, he would later accompany Birbiglia on stand-up tours, which helped him hone his stage skills.

After graduating, John Mulaney moved to New York and was hired as a Comedy Central office assistant. He stayed for a year before pitching the idea of ​​I Love the 30’s as a parody of the I Love the 80’s . He developed the show with his old improviser friend Nick Kroll. Mulaney soon landed an appearance on Saturday Night Live , began contributing to other projects on television such as Documentary Now!, and the Comedy Central Roast of James Franco. John Mulaney soon ventured into appearing as a supporting actor on shows like Difficult People and Crashing.

In 2018, John Mulaney does voice work for the lead character in the Netflix animated series Big Mouth, co-created by Nick Kroll. It was also announced that same year that he would be voicing the character of Spider-Ham for the Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse animated film.

Who is John Mulaney’s wife – Annamarie Tendler?

John Mulaney is married to Annamarie Tendler. Annamarie, who was born on June 9, 1985, is both a makeup artist and a writer. She has managed to combine both passions and has become known for her blog, The Daily Face. She’s also the author of The Daily Face: 25 Makeup For Day, Night And Everything In Between! And Pin It!: 20 Fabulous Bobby Pin Hairstyles.

Mulaney’s wife has also worked in television as a makeup artist and stylist, and owns her own small business called Silk Parlor, where she makes and sells handcrafted Victorian-style lampshades. The two wed on July 5, 2014 at the Onteora Mountain House in Boiceville, New York.

Wealth (income and earnings)

John Mulaney has worked on Saturday Night Life, has numerous show credits to his name and is still credited with comedy and touring. It’s no surprise then that he has an estimated net worth of $3 million and will likely be smiling into the bank for a long time to come.

Other facts

  • John Mulaney once had a drinking problem and gave up drinking.
  • He has had a very extensive career in stand-up comedy and has performed on Jimmy Kimmel Live , and Comedy Central Presents . He also released stand-up comedy albums in 2009 and 2012.
  • John Mulaney was nominated for a Primetime Emmy for Outstanding Writing for a SNL Staff Variety Series from 2009-2012.
  • Along with Justin Timberlake, Seth Meyers and Katreese Barnes, he won a Primetime Emmy for Outstanding Original Music and Lyrics at the 63rd annual Primetime Emmy Awards.
  • He is the third Saturday Night Live writer to host Saturday Night Live without a cast member promotion.
