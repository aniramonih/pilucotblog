Doris Day (Movie Actress) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Doris Day is an American actress, singer and animal rights activist. She began her career as a singer of the big band in 1939, achieving commercial success in 1945 with two recordings No. 1, "Sentimental Journey" and "My Dreams Are. Getting Better All the Time "with The Brown & His Band of Renown. She left Brown to embark on a solo career and recorded over 650 songs between 1947 and 1967. Day was one of the greatest movie stars of the 1950-1960 era. Day's film career began during the heyday of Hollywood with the movie Romance at Sea. She has appeared in films of many genres including musicals, comedies, dramas, and horror films. She starred in Calamity Jane and starred in Alfred Hitchcock's The Man Who Knew Too Much with James Stewart. Her most famous films are those she played with Rock Hudson, the main one being Pillow Talk from 1959, for which she was nominated for an Oscar for Best Actress.
