Top 10 Stunning Punjabi Actresses You Need to Know - SarkariResult

News: Ever heard of Pollywood or Punjwood? That’s the name given to Punjabi cinema. Now, let’s delve into the world of the most captivating Punjabi actresses, whether born in Punjab or not. What matters most is how they’ve dazzled the Punjabi film industry with their exceptional talents. Do you agree?

Behold, our compilation of 15 renowned Punjabi actresses you should be familiar with, ranging from influential figures like Sargun Mehta to the beautiful Ihana Dhillon and the Bigg Boss sensation Shehnaaz Gill. We’ve gathered the crème de la crème of Pollywood in the list below, so why wait? Let’s explore:

1. Sargun Mehta:

Sargun Mehta, the epitome of Punjabi cinema, embarked on her career with theatrical shows and a role in the TV series “12/24 Karol Bagh” on ZeeTV. With two Filmfare Awards Punjabi and three PTC Punjab Film Awards under her belt, Sargun has left an indelible mark in the industry. Noteworthy Punjabi movies in her repertoire include “Angrej,” “Love Punjab,” “Qismat,” “Surkhi Bindi,” “Saunkan Saunkne,” “Jhalle,” and “Chandigarh Amritsar Chandigarh.”

2. Sonam Bajwa:

Born on 16 August 1989, Sonam Bajwa made her debut in Punjabi cinema with “Best of Luck.” She played the lead role of Jeeti in “Punjab 1984” and earned the PTC Punjabi Film Award for “Ardab Mutiyaran.” Beyond Punjabi cinema, she graced Telugu and Tamil films and made appearances in Hindi films such as “Bala” and “Street Dancer 3D.”

3. Surveen Chawla:

The actress famous for “Hate Story 2,” Surveen Chawla, a Chandigarh native born on 1 August 1984, kickstarted her career with the TV serial “Kahin To Hoga.” In the realm of Punjabi films, she has left her mark with movies like “Dharti,” “Taur Mittran Di,” “Singh vs Kaur,” “Hero Naam Yaad Rakhi,” and more. Her accolades include PTC Punjabi Film awards for “Dharti” and “Disco Singh.”

4. Shehnaaz Kaur Gill:

Rising to fame with Bigg Boss Season 13, Shehnaaz Kaur Gill is not just an actress but also a notable singer. Starting her career with the music video “Shiv Di Kitaab,” she has featured in Punjabi movies like “Sat Shri Akaal England,” “Kala Shah Kala,” “Daaka,” and “Honsla Rakh.”

5. Tania:

Born on 6 May 1993, Tania gained fame in Punjabi cinema with her debut in “Qismat.” Her standout performance earned her the Brit Asia TV Award for Best Supporting Actress. Subsequently, she graced other Punjabi films like “Son of Manjeet Singh,” “Guddiyan Patole,” “Sufna,” “Qismat 2,” and “Lekh.”

6. Neeru Bajwa:

A Canadian actress, director, and producer, Neeru Bajwa made her mark in Punjabi Cinema and Bollywood. From soap operas to her Bollywood debut in “Main Solah Baras Ki,” she has an extensive filmography including “Dil Apna Punjabi,” “Jatt & Juliett,” “Laung Laachi,” and more.

7. Mahie Gill:

Born on 19 December 1975 in Chandigarh, Mahie Gill is renowned for her work in Punjabi and Hindi films. Her breakout role in “Dev D” earned her the Filmfare Critics Award for Best Actress. She has also graced Punjabi films like “Carry on Jatta,” “Shareek,” and “Jora: The Second Chapter.”

8. Mandy Takhar:

A British actress with prominence in Punjabi, Indian, and Tamil cinema, Mandy Takhar, born in Wolverhampton, UK, made her debut in “Ekam: Son of Soil” and later landed the lead role in “Mirza: The Untold Story.” She received the PTC Punjabi Film Awards for Best Supporting Actor.

9. Dhriti Saharan:

Known for her work in Punjabi cinema, Dhriti Saharan, born on 10 September 1993 in Kolkata, began her career with “Pure Punjabi.” She gained recognition with the film “Sadda Haq,” winning the Critics Choice Award for Best Actress at PTC Punjabi Film Awards in 2014.

10. Navneet Kaur Dhillon:

Hailing from Ambala, Navneet Kaur Dhillon, born on 23 September 1992, represented India in the Miss World 2013 pageant. Known for her roles in “Love Shhuda,” “Ambarsariya,” and “High-End Yaariyan,” she has left a lasting impression in the industry.
