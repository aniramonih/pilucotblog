Early Life & Biography

Steve Zahn was born on 13 November 1967, in Minnesota, United States, to his parents Carleton Edward Zahn and Zelda Clair Zahn. His father was a Lutheran minister, and his mother was a bookstore clerk and later a YMCA administrator. Zahn spent his childhood in Mankato, and completed his schooling from Kennedy Elementary School and moved to the suburbs of Minneapolis for high school. He completed his graduation from Robbinsdale Cooper High School and later decided to join the United States Marine Corps.

Personal Life

Robyn gave birth to two children, whose names are Henry and Audrey. They are currently residing in Lexington, Kentucky, where they own a 360-acre farmhouse. Career

Zahn made his professional debut in 1987 in Minnesota production of Neil Simon’s movie Biloxi Blues after he became the member of Actor’s Equity. His friends suggested that Zahn’s acting was inspiring that enrolled him into the American Repertory Theatre and worked with several important personalities, one such is director Andrei Serban.

Zahn formed a group of Malaparte theatre company with a group of friends who are actors by profession, which included Ethan Hawke and Robert Sean Leonard. He played the role of Hugo Peabody in the national tour of Bye Bye Birdie and again in two Off-Broadway plays named Sophistry and Eric Bogosian’s Suburbia. Zahn rose to fame after he got a role in the movie ‘Happy, Texas,’ which was directed by Mark Illsley for which he won a Special Jury Award at the Sundance Film Festival. He has also worked in the television, portraying the role of Davis McClary on 36 episodes of HBO’s Treme.
