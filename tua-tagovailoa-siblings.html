Tua Tagovailoa Siblings: Does the Dolphins QB have a brother?

Tua Tagovailoa, the NFL quarterback hails from a knit family, with deep connections to football. While there is knowledge about Tua’s journey and achievements there is also curiosity surrounding his familial bonds, particularly with regards to his siblings. In this article, we will delve into the existence of a brother in Tua Tagovaila’s life. Provide insights, into their relationship if one exists.

Does Tua Tagovailoa have a Brother?

Yes, Tua has a brother named Taulia Tagovailoa who happens to be a college football quarterback. Currently, he plays for the Maryland Terrapins. Taulia is highly regarded in the realm of college football. Is recognized as one of the best quarterbacks, in the nation.

His exceptional attributes include a throwing arm, impressive accuracy, and great mobility. Tua and Taulia share a bond. Have always been supportive of each other’s respective careers. Tua has expressed that Taulia is his fan and Taulia looks up to Tua as his role model.

It’s quite conceivable that Taulia may emulate his brother’s path and eventually make it to the NFL. He possesses all the skills to excel as a quarterback, at the level. Tagovailoa was a freshman, at the University of Alabama in 2019. He had the opportunity to play alongside his brothers, Tua and Mac Jones.

His playing time was limited to just five games. During those games, he managed to complete 9 out of 12 passes for a total of 100 yards and one touchdown. However, in 2020, Tagovailoa decided to transfer to Maryland.

In the four games that he started for his team, he showcased his skills by completing 61.5% of his passes for a total of 1,011 yards. Along, with these stats came seven touchdowns and seven interceptions as well.

Taulia Tagovailoa NIL Deals

As reported by ON3, Taulia Tagovailoa’s endorsement deal, under the NIL (Name, Image, and Likeness) concept is quite remarkable amounting to $372,000. This valuation underscores his growing popularity. Appeal as a quarterback.

Tagovailoa’s endorsement deal serves as proof of his increasing recognition in college football and his ability to connect with fans and sponsors. His agreement includes a combination of compensation, complimentary products, and services as equity stakes in various companies.

He has established partnerships with brands like Roots Natural Kitchen, Tempo College Park, and Jersey Mikes. On top of the NIL deal, Tagovailoa has the potential to earn money from ticket sales, merchandise sales, and future NFL contracts.

Undoubtedly one of the players, in college football nationwide he has the opportunity to accumulate millions through both his NIL ventures and forthcoming professional endeavors.

Who Else is in the Tua Tagovailoa Family?

Tua Tagovailoa comes from a family of Samoan heritage being the oldest, among four siblings. Both of his parents, Galu and Diane Tagovailoa were raised in Hawaii. Have roots in American Samoa. Galu, Tua’s father used to be a powerlifter and football coach.

He played a role in introducing football to Tua and guiding him throughout his years in the sport. As for Diane, Tua’s mother, she has always been a homemaker who encouraged her son’s football aspirations. Tua also has three siblings named Taulia, Taylor, and Taysia. Tua is married to his wife Annah and they have one son, Ace.
