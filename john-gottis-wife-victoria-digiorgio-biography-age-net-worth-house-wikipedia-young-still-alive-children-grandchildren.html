Biography

Victoria DiGiorgio (born 5 December 1942), also addressed as Victoria Gotti, is a renowned writer and businesswoman from the United States. John Gotti, a prominent Italian-American gangster who rose to control New York City’s famed Gambino crime family, left behind a widow named Victoria DiGiorgio. The outspoken but composed she first encountered the criminal at a bar, where they began a romance that eventually led to marriage. 

While raising their five children, Victoria Gotti, Angel Gotti, Frank Gotti, Peter Gotti Jr., and John A. Gotti, the couple lived a lower-middle-class lifestyle and upheld traditional values. Unfortunately, while riding his friend’s mini-bike, Frank Gotti was struck and murdered by a car driven by his neighbour John Favara.

Victoria DiGiorgio suffered from depression as a result of this tragedy, and he was bedridden for a year. Four months after Frank Gotti‘s passing, Favara vanished, and it is assumed that John Gotti gave the order for his execution. 

When Victoria DiGiorgio learned that her son John A. Gotti was also implicated in his father’s shady dealings, her marriage to John Gotti began to crumble. John A. Gotti is thought to have served as the Gambino crime family’s leader from 1992 (the year John Gotti was sent behind bars) until 1999.
