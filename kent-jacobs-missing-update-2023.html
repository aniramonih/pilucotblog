Kent Jacobs Missing Update 2023

A week before his 42nd birthday, on March 10, 2002, Kent Jacobs disappeared in mysterious circumstances.

Jackie, Kent’s sister, recounted the terrible turn of events, saying that they had been preparing a happy celebration for him but that it resulted in one of the most thorough searches in Cumberland County’s history.

Kent was last seen wearing a black Harley Davidson hoodie, blue trousers, and black sneakers, about two miles from his mother Martha’s house.

 

Local officials started investigating Kent’s disappearance, which became a homicide inquiry. Kent was subsequently ruled legally dead by the Cumberland County Superior Court on September 10, 2012.

Kent Jacobs, 42, mysteriously vanished from his mother’s house in Hope Mills, North Carolina, in March 2002, according to the most recent Dateline podcast, “Missing in America.”

According to reports, he was a special needs boy en route to see his pals when he vanished. Despite the best efforts of the police over the next two decades, he was never found.

Who Was Kent Jacobs?

Kent Jacobs, a 42-year-old Hope Mills, North Carolina inhabitant, was the first child born to local small business owners Martha and Calvin “Jack” Jacobs.

Kent’s mental ability had not improved since the age of nine, according to his family, who classified him as having a moderate developmental disability.

Kimberly, Kent’s younger sister, said he had a permanent innocence of a child that was both charming and appealing.

She told the Fayetteville Observer that he was always so innocent and gorgeous. She hoped to continue having the same outlook on life even without him.

According to his sister Jackie, Kent’s absence significantly affected their family. Every day, I think about him. Every day, I love him,” she said.

She compared the experience to moving from an energetic, colorful environment to one that is now dark. She made a point of highlighting the enormous extent of the ongoing emotional ordeal her family has gone through.

Is Carolina Missing Man Kent Jacobs Found Yet?

Kent Jacobs is still missing. The officials carried out a thorough search, but they have yet to find anything.

The Cumberland County Police Department is handling Kent’s case, and according to Detective Patrice Bogertey, the department is still looking for Kent.

Kent’s death was officially declared in 2012 by a judge, but his family and the police have not given up.

One of the final locations Kent was spotted strolling through on his Sunday visits with boyhood pals was 5437 Jackson St., where police recently conducted a failed three-day search in late April 2023.

Exclusive to the program, Jackie said she is offering a cash prize of $15,000 to anyone with information leading to Kent’s whereabouts.

If it results in the capture and conviction of those accountable for her brother’s abduction, she also offers a $100,000 prize.

The Kent Jacobs Foundation was a nonprofit organization the family established to aid people in comparable circumstances.
